Absolute value If \(a\) is a real number, the absolute value (or magnitude) of \(a\text{,}\) denoted \(\lvert a\rvert\text{,}\) is the distance between \(a\) and zero on the number line. If \(a\) and \(b\) are real numbers, the absolute value of their difference, denoted \(\lvert a-b\rvert\text{,}\) is the distance between \(a\) and \(b\) on the number line.
