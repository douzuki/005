Solving a linear equation in one variable
  1. Clear fractions Multiply each term (on both sides of the equation) by the LCD of the denominators (or the product of the denominators)
  2. Simplify both sides
  3. Move all \(x\) terms to one side of the other equation and constants to the other side.
  4. Divide both sides by the coefficient of \(x\text{.}\)
